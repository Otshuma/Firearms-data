THE OTHERSIDE OF FIREARMS

By Osmond Tshuma

The issue of firearm ownership stands as a prominent and contentious subject in America, encompassing discussions ranging from safety concerns to the distressing surge in school shootings. Recent reports from CNN indicate that there have been at least 80 school shootings in the United States as of December 6 this year. However, this analysis takes a specific focus on firearm suicides and homicides from 1949 to 2020.

As an international student, hailing from a background where encounters with real firearms have been rare—perhaps occurring only twice in my entire life—I embark on the endeavor of tackling a data narrative project that delves into a sensitive and challenging topic. It is both a brave and somewhat naive undertaking, exploring the intricate web of factors surrounding firearm-related incidents in the United States.

The primary objective of this project is to discern whether there exists a comparative or intrinsic link between firearm suicides and firearm homicides during the period under examination. Through a thoughtful and data-driven approach, this analysis aims to contribute to understanding of the complexities surrounding firearm-related incidents in American society.
